Cosmological Sensitivity of :math:`\Delta\Sigma` via Finite Differences
========================================================================

This page shows how a 1% change in each cosmological parameter propagates into
the galaxy–galaxy lensing signal :math:`\Delta\Sigma(R)` across the scale range
:math:`R \in [0.01,\,30]\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, using the **full 1-halo + 2-halo
halo model** (:class:`~hod_mod.galaxies.clustering.FullHaloModelPrediction`,
More+2015, `arXiv:1407.1856 <https://arxiv.org/abs/1407.1856>`_) with an NFW
profile and Diemer+19 concentration–mass relation.

.. math::

    \mathcal{S}_\theta(R)
      = \frac{\mathrm{d}\ln\Delta\Sigma(R)}{\mathrm{d}\ln\theta}
      \approx \frac{\Delta\Sigma(R;\,\theta+\delta\theta)
                    - \Delta\Sigma(R;\,\theta-\delta\theta)}
                   {2\,\delta\theta/\theta \times \Delta\Sigma(R;\,\theta_{\rm fid})}

All figures and the table below are generated by
``hod_mod/scripts/sensitivity/demo_sensitivity_ds.py``::

    python hod_mod/scripts/sensitivity/demo_sensitivity_ds.py \
        --output results/showcase/fig_sensitivity_ds

.. note::

   At :math:`R \lesssim 0.02\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, the discrete tabulation grid
   (starting at :math:`R_{\min} = 0.01\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`) causes a numerical
   sign flip in :math:`\Delta\Sigma` because the cumulative mean
   :math:`\bar{\Sigma}(<R)` cannot be computed below the grid boundary.  Values
   at :math:`R = 0.01\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` are shown for completeness but should
   not be interpreted physically.  For :math:`R \geq 0.03\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`,
   the 1h+2h prediction gives physically correct, positive :math:`\Delta\Sigma`.

1. The Logarithmic Response Function
--------------------------------------

The fiducial model uses **BGS M10 MAP best-fit parameters** from the
LS10 DR10 multi-probe fit (:math:`r_p > 0.3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`):
Planck 2018 ΛCDM, Tinker+2008 HMF, and Zheng+2007 HOD
at the BGS M10 effective redshift :math:`z_{\rm eff} = 0.136`.

   at large scales (2-halo regime).

2. Comparison with :math:`w_p`
--------------------------------

:math:`\Delta\Sigma` and :math:`w_p` probe different combinations of the
galaxy–halo connection:

- :math:`w_p \propto P_{gg}(k) = b_{\rm eff}^2\,P_{\rm lin}(k)` (2-halo)
- :math:`\Delta\Sigma \propto P_{gm}(k) = b_{\rm eff}\,P_{\rm lin}(k)` (2-halo)

The ratio :math:`\Delta\Sigma / w_p \propto 1/b_{\rm eff}` is therefore
**bias-free**, making the combination of the two probes particularly powerful
(SNR forecast page coming soon).

This propagates directly into the logarithmic responses:

Key contrasts with the 1h+2h model:

- **:math:`\sigma_8`**: Both probes now show large, scale-dependent responses
  dominated by the 1-halo term at intermediate scales.
  :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}^{w_p}` peaks at :math:`+8.7` near
  :math:`r_p \sim 1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` while
  :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}^{\Delta\Sigma}` peaks at :math:`+9.6` near
  :math:`R \sim 3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`.  The higher peak for :math:`\Delta\Sigma`
  reflects the lensing projection which accumulates power over a wider LOS range.

- **:math:`\Omega_m`**: At :math:`R = 1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`,
  :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}^{\Delta\Sigma} = +2.74` (positive) while
  :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}^{w_p} = +1.84` (positive).  Both probes
  are now positive at this scale (1-halo term dominates).  The sign contrast
  previously seen at this scale in the 2-halo-only model was a 2-halo artifact.
  At large scales (:math:`R = 30\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`), :math:`\Delta\Sigma` remains
  more positive than :math:`w_p`, which becomes negative beyond
  :math:`r_p \sim 20\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`.

- **:math:`h`** and **:math:`n_s`**: Both remain positive at intermediate scales
  for :math:`\Delta\Sigma`.  At large scales (:math:`r_p \gtrsim 30\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`)
  both probes show negative :math:`\mathcal{S}_{n_s}`, reflecting the suppression
  of large-scale power by a tilted spectrum.

3. Physical Interpretation
---------------------------

**:math:`\sigma_8` (amplitude)** —
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}` is strongly scale-dependent with the
1h+2h model.  For :math:`R \geq 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` (physically
meaningful regime), it rises from :math:`+0.26` at
:math:`R = 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` to a peak of :math:`\approx +9.6` near
:math:`R \sim 3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` where the 1-halo number density
(:math:`\propto n(M_{\rm host})` with steep :math:`\sigma_8` dependence
through the HMF) dominates, then levels off to :math:`\approx +6.0` at
large scales (2-halo regime).  This contrasts sharply with the
2-halo-only value of :math:`1.79`, which was scale-independent.
The higher peak than :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}^{w_p} \approx 8.7`
reflects the lensing projection, which accumulates 1-halo power over a
wider line-of-sight range.

**:math:`\Omega_m` (matter density)** —
For :math:`R \geq 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`,
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}^{\Delta\Sigma}` is positive and
scale-dependent: :math:`+0.59` at :math:`R = 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`,
rising to a peak of :math:`+3.34` near :math:`R \sim 3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`
(1-halo dominated), then decreasing to :math:`+1.86` at
:math:`R = 30\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`.  Because :math:`\Delta\Sigma` involves
one fewer power of bias than :math:`w_p \propto b_{\rm eff}^2 P_{\rm lin}`,
the large-scale "anti-correlation" from :math:`k_{\rm eq}` shifting is
weaker, keeping :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}^{\Delta\Sigma}` positive
even at :math:`R = 30\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`.

**:math:`h` (Hubble constant)** —
For :math:`R \geq 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`,
:math:`\mathcal{S}_h^{\Delta\Sigma}` rises from :math:`+0.15` at
:math:`R = 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` to :math:`+3.65` near
:math:`R \sim 3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, then decreases to :math:`+1.78` at
:math:`R = 30\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, remaining positive throughout.
The strong 1-halo contribution near :math:`3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` amplifies
the response relative to the 2-halo-only prediction.

**:math:`n_s` (spectral index)** —
For :math:`R \geq 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`,
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{n_s}^{\Delta\Sigma}` rises from :math:`+0.11` at
:math:`R = 0.1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` to :math:`+1.02` at
:math:`R \sim 3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, then crosses zero near
:math:`R \sim 20\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` and reaches :math:`-0.33` at
:math:`R = 30\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, reflecting the tilting of power between
small and large scales.

5. From Response Functions to Fisher Information
--------------------------------------------------

The :math:`\mathcal{S}_\theta(R)` arrays are the inputs to the Fisher
matrix analysis (SNR forecast page coming soon), which will show how combining
:math:`\Delta\Sigma` with :math:`w_p` dramatically reduces the required
signal-to-noise ratio for measuring :math:`\Omega_m` and :math:`\sigma_8`.
