HOD Parameter Sensitivity of :math:`w_p` — All Models
=======================================================

This page extends the cosmological sensitivity analysis (see
:doc:`autodiff_sensitivity`) to **all HOD parameters** and **six HOD
parametrizations** fitted to the BGS M10 sample.  Sensitivities are computed
via numerical **centred finite differences** (1% relative step) through the
full **1-halo + 2-halo** halo model (:class:`~hod_mod.galaxies.clustering.FullHaloModelPrediction`,
More+2015), identical to the single-model analysis in :doc:`autodiff_sensitivity`.

**Parameters**: best-fit MAP values from the BGS M10 LS10 DR10 multi-probe fit
(:math:`r_p > 0.3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, :math:`z_{\rm eff} = 0.136`), stored in
``results/bgs_multiprobe/mstar10.0_wp_{model}_nfw_rp300/map_result.json``.

All figures and tables are generated by
``hod_mod/scripts/sensitivity/demo_sensitivity_all_hod.py``::

    python hod_mod/scripts/sensitivity/demo_sensitivity_all_hod.py \
        --output results/showcase/fig_sensitivity

Overview: What Changes Across HOD Models
------------------------------------------

The logarithmic response of :math:`w_p(r_p)` to a cosmological parameter
:math:`\theta_{\rm cosmo}` is **model-dependent**: different HOD
parametrizations set different galaxy–halo connection thresholds and
therefore probe different halo mass ranges, shifting the effective bias and
the 2-halo amplitude.  The table below summarises the fiducial
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{\theta}(r_p)` at five representative scales for each
model.

Key observations:

- :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}` is now **strongly scale-dependent** with the
  1h+2h model: it peaks near :math:`r_p \sim 1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` at
  :math:`\approx 8.6\text{–}9.1` (all models) and levels off to
  :math:`\approx 5.1\text{–}5.3` at large scales.  The 2-halo-only values
  (which ranged from 0.17 to 2.81 across models) are now superseded by the
  dominant 1-halo contribution.
- :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}` is now **near model-independent** at 1 Mpc/h,
  varying only ±3% across all six parametrizations — the 1-halo term is
  insensitive to the detailed HOD functional form.
- :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}` and :math:`\mathcal{S}_{n_s}` remain nearly
  model-independent, driven by :math:`P(k)` shape.
- :math:`\mathcal{S}_{h}` changes sign near :math:`r_p \sim 40\text{–}50\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`
  for all models, with small model-to-model variation.

Universal Cosmological Degeneracies
-------------------------------------

Across **all** eight models, the same three cosmological parameter pairs are
degenerate in their effect on the *shape* of :math:`w_p(r_p)`:

.. list-table:: Degenerate cosmological pairs — universal across all HOD models
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 20 15 65

   * - Pair
     - Pearson :math:`r`
     - Physical interpretation
   * - :math:`h` ↔ :math:`\Omega_m`
     - +0.996
     - Both shift :math:`k_{\rm eq}` and the BAO scale;
       near-perfect positive correlation in :math:`\mathcal{S}` shape
   * - :math:`h` ↔ :math:`n_s`
     - +0.926
     - Both affect the tilt/pivot of :math:`P(k)` on sub-BAO scales
   * - :math:`\Omega_m` ↔ :math:`n_s`
     - +0.905
     - Strongly correlated but not fully degenerate; :math:`n_s` has a
       wider dynamic range (:math:`\pm 4`) vs :math:`\Omega_m` (:math:`-3.5` to :math:`+0.5`)

These degeneracies are broken by **amplitude** information (:math:`\sigma_8`)
or by external priors (CMB, BAO).

Per-Model Sensitivity and Degeneracy Structure
------------------------------------------------

Each figure below shows two panels for one HOD model:

- **Left** — Response curves :math:`\mathcal{S}_\theta(r_p)` for all parameters.
  Cosmological parameters (thick lines); HOD parameters (thin lines, grey tones).
- **Right** — Pearson correlation matrix of response vectors across all
  :math:`r_p` scales.  Stars (★) mark :math:`|r| \geq 0.99`; diamonds (◆) mark
  :math:`|r| \geq 0.95`.  A high correlation means the two parameters are
  **degenerate** in :math:`w_p(r_p)` alone.

Implications for Parameter Inference
--------------------------------------

The correlation analysis leads to three practical conclusions for fitting
:math:`w_p(r_p)` data:

1. **Fix or externally constrain** :math:`h` if only :math:`w_p` is available
   — it is degenerate with both :math:`\Omega_m` (:math:`r = 0.996`) and
   :math:`n_s` (:math:`r = 0.926`) in all HOD models.  A CMB or BAO prior
   on :math:`h` fully breaks these degeneracies.

2. **Combine** :math:`w_p` with :math:`\Delta\Sigma(R)` to break the
   :math:`\sigma_8`–bias degeneracy.  The lensing signal scales as
   :math:`b_{\rm eff}\,\sigma_8^2` while :math:`w_p \propto b_{\rm eff}^2\,\sigma_8^2`,
   so the ratio :math:`\Delta\Sigma / w_p \propto \sigma_8^2 / b_{\rm eff}`
   constrains both simultaneously.

3. **HOD model choice has little effect on the :math:`\sigma_8` lever-arm**
   at :math:`r_p \lesssim 3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`: with the full 1h+2h model,
   all six parametrizations give :math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8} \approx 8.6\text{–}9.1`
   at :math:`r_p = 1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`.  The 1-halo term is much more
   sensitive to the total matter content than to HOD functional form,
   so the :math:`\sigma_8` constraint is robust to HOD misspecification.

The sensitivity curves are the inputs to the Fisher matrix for the full
SNR forecasting recipe (coming soon).
