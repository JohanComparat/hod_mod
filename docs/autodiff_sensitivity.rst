Cosmological Sensitivity of :math:`w_p` via Finite Differences
===============================================================

This page shows how a 1% change in each cosmological parameter propagates into
the projected correlation function :math:`w_p(r_p)` across the full scale range
:math:`r_p \in [0.01,\,60]\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, using the **full 1-halo + 2-halo
halo model** (More+2015, `arXiv:1407.1011 <https://arxiv.org/abs/1407.1011>`_)
with an NFW profile and Diemer+19 concentrationŌĆōmass relation.

Sensitivities are computed as centred finite differences (step size
:math:`\delta\theta/\theta = 1\%`):

.. math::

    \mathcal{S}_\theta(r_p)
      \approx \frac{w_p(r_p;\,\theta+\delta\theta)
                    - w_p(r_p;\,\theta-\delta\theta)}
                   {2\,\delta\theta\,/\,\theta \times w_p(r_p;\,\theta_{\rm fid})}

This captures all cosmology dependences through the 1-halo integrals
(HMF, NFW amplitude), the 2-halo effective bias, and the EH98 power spectrum.
The concentrationŌĆōmass relation is evaluated at the fiducial MAP cosmology and
held fixed during the perturbations.

All figures and the table below are generated by
``hod_mod/scripts/sensitivity/demo_sensitivity_wp.py``::

    python hod_mod/scripts/sensitivity/demo_sensitivity_wp.py \
        --output results/showcase/fig_sensitivity_wp

1. The Logarithmic Response Function
--------------------------------------

The **logarithmic response** of an observable :math:`f` to a parameter
:math:`\theta` is

.. math::

    \mathcal{S}_\theta(r_p)
      \;=\; \frac{\mathrm{d}\ln f}{\mathrm{d}\ln\theta}
      \;=\; \frac{\theta}{f}\,\frac{\partial f}{\partial\theta}

so that a fractional change :math:`\Delta\theta/\theta = 1\%` in :math:`\theta`
produces a change :math:`\mathcal{S}_\theta \times 1\%` in :math:`f`.
For :math:`f = w_p(r_p)` and :math:`\theta \in \{\Omega_m,\,\sigma_8,\,h,\,n_s\}`:

.. math::

    \mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}(r_p)
      &= \frac{\Omega_m}{w_p(r_p)}\,\frac{\partial w_p(r_p)}{\partial\Omega_m}
        \\[4pt]
    \mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}(r_p)
      &= \frac{2}{w_p(r_p)}\,
         \frac{\partial w_p(r_p)}{\partial\ln\!A_s}
        \quad\bigl[\sigma_8 \propto A_s^{1/2},\;
                   \mathrm{d}\ln\sigma_8 = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{d}(\ln A_s)\bigr]
        \\[4pt]
    \mathcal{S}_h(r_p)
      &= \frac{h}{w_p(r_p)}\,\frac{\partial w_p(r_p)}{\partial h}
        \\[4pt]
    \mathcal{S}_{n_s}(r_p)
      &= \frac{n_s}{w_p(r_p)}\,\frac{\partial w_p(r_p)}{\partial n_s}

The fiducial model uses **BGS M10 MAP best-fit parameters** from the
LS10 DR10 multi-probe fit (:math:`r_p > 0.3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`):
Planck 2018 ╬øCDM (`arXiv:1807.06209 <https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.06209>`_),
Tinker+2008 HMF (`arXiv:0803.2706 <https://arxiv.org/abs/0803.2706>`_),
and Zheng+2007 HOD (`arXiv:astro-ph/0703457 <https://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/0703457>`_)
at the BGS M10 effective redshift :math:`z_{\rm eff} = 0.136`.

   then levels off to :math:`\approx 5.2` at large scales (2-halo regime).

2. Physical Interpretation
---------------------------

**:math:`\sigma_8` (amplitude)** ŌĆö
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{\sigma_8}` is strongly scale-dependent with the 1h+2h model.
It is negative at :math:`r_p = 0.01\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` (:math:`-0.70`) where the
1-halo profile shape causes a sign flip, rises to a peak of :math:`\approx +8.7`
near :math:`r_p \sim 1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` where the 1-halo number density
(:math:`\propto n(M_{\rm host})` with steep :math:`\sigma_8` dependence through
the HMF) dominates, then levels off to :math:`\approx +5.2` at large scales (2-halo
regime, :math:`w_p \propto b_{\rm eff}^2 P_{\rm lin} \propto \sigma_8^{>2}`).
This contrasts with the 2-halo-only value of 1.58, which was scale-independent.

**:math:`\Omega_m` (matter density)** ŌĆö
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{\Omega_m}` is negative at small scales
(:math:`-0.79` at :math:`r_p = 0.01\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`), passes through zero
near :math:`r_p \approx 0.3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, peaks at :math:`+1.8` near
:math:`r_p \sim 0.3\text{ŌĆō}1\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`, then becomes increasingly
negative at large scales (ŌłÆ1.6 at 60 Mpc/h).
At fixed :math:`A_s`, increasing :math:`\Omega_m` shifts the matter-radiation
equality wavenumber :math:`k_{\rm eq} \propto \Omega_m` to smaller scales,
suppressing large-scale power and hence :math:`w_p` at large :math:`r_p`.

**:math:`h` (Hubble constant)** ŌĆö
:math:`\mathcal{S}_h` decreases from :math:`+3.0` at :math:`r_p = 0.3\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`
to :math:`-0.22` at :math:`r_p = 60\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`.  The sign change near
:math:`r_p \sim 40\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}` marks a pivot scale.

**:math:`n_s` (spectral index)** ŌĆö
:math:`\mathcal{S}_{n_s}` changes sign from negative at small scales (:math:`-0.67`
at :math:`r_p = 0.01\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`) to slightly positive at intermediate scales
(peak :math:`\approx +0.13`) then negative again at large scales
(:math:`-3.0` at :math:`r_p = 60\,h^{-1}{\rm Mpc}`), reflecting the tilting of
power between small and large scales.

4. From Response Functions to Fisher Information
--------------------------------------------------

The logarithmic response is the first ingredient of the Fisher information
matrix.  Given a data vector
:math:`\mathbf{w}_p = [w_p(r_{p,1}),\ldots,w_p(r_{p,N})]`
with uniform fractional noise :math:`\sigma_k = w_{p,k}/{\rm SNR}_{\rm bin}`:

.. math::

    G_{ij} = \sum_k \mathcal{S}_{\theta_i}(r_{p,k})\,\mathcal{S}_{\theta_j}(r_{p,k})

    \sigma(\ln\theta_i)^{-1}_{\rm unmarg} = {\rm SNR}_{\rm bin}\,\sqrt{G_{ii}}

The full Fisher analysis combining :math:`w_p` and :math:`\Delta\Sigma` is on
the SNR forecast page (coming soon).
